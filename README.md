## What Is a Clone Sender Attack?

A **clone sender** attack — also called **clone phishing** — is a tactic where an attacker impersonates a known contact or brand by copying a legitimate, previously received email and resending a near-identical replica. Usually only the links or attachments are swapped for malicious ones, while the layout, branding, and writing style stay exactly the same.

## How Clone Phishing Works

1. **Interception** — The attacker compromises an email account or otherwise gains access to a real piece of correspondence.
2. **Duplication** — They copy the exact layout, images, branding, and tone of a legitimate email (e.g. an invoice, parcel tracking update, or internal IT memo).
3. **Spoofing** — They alter the `From` address, display name, or `Reply-To` address so the message looks like it came from the original sender.
4. **The Hook** — The cloned message includes a pretext such as "There was an error in the original document, please use this updated link" to get the recipient to click a malicious link or download malware.

## Why Clone Phishing Works So Well

Because the cloned email reuses real branding and context the recipient has already seen, it lowers natural suspicion — making people more likely to act on the malicious link or attachment without double-checking.

## FAQ

**What is the difference between clone phishing and regular phishing?**
Regular phishing emails are often generic. Clone phishing reuses a real, previously seen email almost exactly, which makes it harder to detect.
